# 15 — Copy Framework

How to write copy for this site. Not the full body copy (that’s migrated and authored by CNZ), but the framework — the principles, patterns, page archetypes, and example copy for new content.

## 22.1 Voice (compact restatement)

Clear, direct, respectful, bilingual-aware, plain English, specific not hype. See section 4.4 for full voice notes.

## 22.2 Page-archetype copy templates

### 22.2.1 Content page (guidance/policy)

Structure:

1. **Title.** Noun phrase describing content. "How to apply for an Arts Grant". NOT "Ready to take the leap?".
1. **Intro (1-2 sentences).** Who this page is for and what it covers. "This page explains how to prepare and submit an application for an Arts Grant. It’s for individual artists and small groups."
1. **H2 sections.** Each is a noun phrase. "Before you start", "What you’ll need", "How to apply", "What happens next", "If you need help".
1. **Closing signpost.** What to do next — a specific link or CTA. Not a generic "We hope this helps!".

### Example: "Before you apply" page

> **Note:** **Example copy**
> Before you apply
> This page lists the steps to take before you start an application. Going through this now saves time later — and improves your chances of a successful application.
> H2 — Check you’re eligible
> Every fund has its own eligibility. The most common requirements are: you’re a New Zealand citizen or permanent resident; you’ve been working as an artist for at least two years; you haven’t recently received funding from the same programme.
> See the fund page for specific eligibility rules.
> H2 — Read the Terms and Conditions
> All CNZ funding comes with standard terms and conditions. These cover reporting, acknowledging CNZ, and what happens if your project changes. You accept these when you submit an application.
> Read the Terms and Conditions of Funding.
> H2 — Set up your portal account
> Applications are made through the CNZ portal. If you’ve never used it, allow 15 minutes to set up an account and add your profile. You don’t need to do this again for future applications.
> Set up your portal account.
> H2 — Talk with an adviser if you’re not sure
> If you’re new to applying, or your project is unusual, talk with an arts adviser first. They can help you shape the application — they can’t write it for you, but they can save you from common mistakes.
> Find your arts adviser.

### 22.2.2 Fund detail page

Structure (follows section 19.4 wireframe):

1. Fund name — just the name. No tagline.
1. Status and close date.
1. Key facts strip.
1. Overview — 2-3 sentences. What the fund exists for.
1. Who can apply — bulleted checklist-style eligibility.
1. What we fund — specific with examples.
1. What we don’t fund — equally specific.
1. How to apply — numbered steps.
1. Assessment criteria — explicit, with weightings if applicable.
1. FAQs — real ones from actual inbound questions.

### Example: Arts Grants fund detail

> **Note:** **Example copy**
> Arts Grants
> Round 3 • Closes 5pm NZT, 12 June 2026
> Overview
> Arts Grants support individual artists and small groups to create new work, develop their practice, or present existing work to audiences. We run three rounds each year.
> Who can apply
> You can apply for an Arts Grant if:
> • You’re a New Zealand citizen or permanent resident.
> • You’ve been working as an artist for at least two years.
> • You’re applying as an individual or a small group of up to five people.
> • You haven’t received an Arts Grant in the past 12 months.
> What we fund
> • Costs of creating new work (artist fees, materials, space, collaborators).
> • Costs of developing your practice (mentorship, residencies, training).
> • Costs of presenting work (venue hire, marketing, accessibility costs).
> What we don’t fund
> • Capital costs or equipment purchases over $5,000.
> • Completed work (we fund work that hasn’t started yet).
> • Degree-level study at tertiary institutions.
> • GST on artist fees (you claim this through IRD).
> How to apply
> 1. Read these guidelines in full.
> 2. Draft your proposal — use our template.
> 3. Gather support material (up to 10 items).
> 4. Submit on the CNZ portal before 5pm NZT, 12 June 2026.
> What happens next
> We assess your application within eight weeks. We’ll let you know the outcome by email, whether successful or not. If you’re funded, we’ll send a funding agreement to sign.
> Assessment criteria
> Applications are scored against:
> • Quality of the proposal — 40%
> • Your experience and track record — 30%
> • Feasibility — 20%
> • Reach and impact — 10%
> Contact
> If you have questions, email Anaïs Whiu (anais.whiu@creativenz.govt.nz).

## 22.4 Microcopy rules

- **Dates:** "12 April 2026". "Today". "Yesterday". "3 days ago". "12 April 2026" again after 7 days.
- **Times:** "5pm", "9:30am". Always specify tz in external-facing deadlines: "5pm NZT".
- **File sizes:** "(PDF, 240KB)". Round to nearest KB or MB.
- **Phone:** "+64 4 473 0880". International format. No spaces after "+64".
- **Currency:** "$10,000". NZD implied. Prefix with "NZ$" or suffix with " NZD" when confusion possible.
- **Percentages:** "40%", not "40 percent".
- **Ranges:** "2-10 years" (en dash if possible, hyphen otherwise). "$10,000-$50,000".
- **Capitalisation:** Sentence case for everything except proper nouns and CNZ programme names. Not Title Case Headings.
